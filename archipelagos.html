Tanayu Archipelagos

What is a Tanayu archipelago?

Tanayu naturally form groups called archipelagos. These take a V-formation with one tanayu at the front that guides the rest along a migratory cycle. Some nations live on these archipelagos and travel the ocean with it. The main tanayu is more often than not where the capital is located.
People have learned to harvest the tadpole algae without compromising the ecosystem for food and fuel, and have mastered the art of fishing. They study the path of their archipelago and which resources the ocean floor will make available for them at each point.

Politics atop the archipelagos

Societies on archipelagos are normally hive nations, each person has a set function. There are divisions dedicated to taking care of the Tanayu and their offspring, harvesting food, diving for resources, defense, and much more. One of the main interests of these nations is growing by acquiring more Tanayu. This is why, during the Tanayu mating season when all archipelagos come together in a common breeding ground, they will try to steal offspring from other nations. This results in battles and wars of considerable proportions, with people specified in underwater combat for this precise occasion.
Tanayu follow their mother until they have younglings of their own, at which point they will swim away and start their own archipelago. This makes it hard for an archipelago to grow big and last long.
