Harbors

What is a harbor?

Harbors are village or city sized artificial constructions that are built in shallow areas to stand above the surface of the water. They donâ€™t have albasoil so they cannot depend on tadpole algae for food or fuel. Instead, they will cultivate and harvest other algae that grow on the seafloor. They are also the only ones capable of building mines by walling off and bailing out a closed area of the sea to extract fuel and metals.
The lack of dangerous depths makes it easy to gather other resources in the area and offers protection from the more dangerous predators, though not all of them.
They work as city states but often work together with other nearby harbors for strength and numbers. Sometimes they even join under a single rule.
While archipelagos are often more homogenous when it comes to their inhabitants, harbors are more likely to be shared by different species and ethnicities.
Archipelago based nations often take boats to nearby harbors when their Tanayu are on one of their days-long sleep to trade the resources they have gathered from far away parts of the world.
